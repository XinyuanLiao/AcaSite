About Me

I received a B.Eng. degree in Computer Science and Technology from Ocean University of China in 2022 and received an M.S. degree in Information and Communication Engineering from Northwestern Polytechnical University in 2025. Currently, I am a Ph.D. student at the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU), jointly supervised by Prof. Zhang Yi and Prof. Liu Junwen. My research interests mainly focus on physics-informed machine learning for wide-bandgap (WBG) semiconductors reliability assessment. In addition, I also interested in the foundation model for power electronics.

Research Interests

Physics-Informed Machine Learning

Using physics-based models to guide machine learning models, improving their generalization and interpretability in engineering applications.

Power Electronics Reliability

Studying the long-term performance and reliability of power electronic devices and systems, including failure prediction and optimization.

Foundation Model

Developing large pre-trained models that can be fine-tuned for a variety of downstream tasks, improving performance across domains.
